What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

To balance my parenting and learning, I already have some plans for Foundations and Bootcamp. While in foundations, I'm splitting my work into blocks of hours and shuffling my parenting responsibilities around them. I aim to study no less than 40 hours per week and spare a weekend day to enjoy my family. For the Bootcamp, I've arranged with my partner to cover the daily daycare drop-off and pick-up so I could work uninterrupted for longer.

Communication is vital to working productively with others. I plan to keep my team and facilitator informed of my schedule and use the appropriate discord status when logged in.

Regarding what I plan to do when things get complicated, I plan to take a step back, have a break, and consider possible solutions. I'll assess if I can solve the problem on my own or if I need help from others. I'll reach out to my facilitator and have a talk about it as I'm sure they've been through something similar or have helped someone with the same. I'm also planning to care about my well-being by exercising. Last year I suffered from postpartum depression, and exercising was vital in my recovery, so I want to keep myself as active as possible.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way:

I plan to ask for help using the appropriate discord channels during foundations and ask for help from the facilitators and other students in person during Bootcamp. Although I want to find the solution on my own, I promise not to go down the rabbit hole and waste too much time if I don't see the answer. I can ask the question and still explore a solution and then compare.

My long-term goal and career pathway

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

Regarding strengths, I can say that I have improved my studying habits. Foundations gave me time to catch up with my rusty studying habits and work on new ones. Using the Pomodoro Technique, I can now focus for shorter periods and work more efficiently. Also, these past weeks grew my confidence because I got to talk to a facilitator about my fears regarding Bootcamp and found that she felt like me before, and she pulled through.

A limitation for me might be my parenting commitments. For instance, my baby sometimes has terrible nights and wakes up several times at night, and I can’t get a good sleep, which might hinder me from being 100% focused that day on campus.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

It might be speaking in public. I tend to be uncomfortable when all eyes are on me and speaking in front of a group, so I think it will be a challenge. However, I know Bootcamp is a safe place, and I look forward to participating as much as possible when it’s my time sharing or speaking.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I want to keep improving my conscious listening as I discovered it’s not my forte, and I want to be more empathetic. I’d also like to see my fixed mindset traits changed into growth ones, especially changing my mind when facing challenges.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

I expect the Bootcamp staff to be motivated tutors who care about the student's learning journey. Who are approachable and empathetic when we reach out for help and guide us to get the answers rather than solving things for us.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp ?

Knowing myself, I might get overwhelmed if I don’t understand a concept, and we quickly move on to something new. I plan to ask as many questions as I need to the tutor and fellow students and ensure I understand them. Also, it’d be a good idea to reflect on and review the information learnt when I get home every day.

I also know that we have support from the human skills team, and I’d book a one of one session if I can’t handle my anxiety or feel too overwhelmed at any point. I don’t want to get to a burnout moment before asking for help.

My Bootcamp commitments are:

  • Although we all know it’s compulsory to wear a mask, I commit myself to wearing it properly and stay at home if I feel sick or have any cold or flu symptoms. I’m sure the facilitators will sort something out to help me participate in the classes and projects.
  • I will be dependable and stick to my word regarding project deadlines, and in the case, I can’t complete the work on time, I’ll timely reach out for help and communicate this to my team and facilitator.
  • I’ll be respectful, empathetic and open-minded when working in pairs/groups and listen consciously to their ideas and approaches.
  • I’ll be considerate to the Bootcamp staff and teachers showing up on time and communicating appropriately.
  • I plan to focus on studying, do as much work as possible on campus, and finish any extra work after my baby is down at night. I’ll make sure to keep my group mates posted on my daily schedule to complete any work needed.
